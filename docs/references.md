# References

This page collects the main sources cited throughout the documentation.

## Chapman 1931

[Chapman 1931](#chapman-1931): Sydney Chapman, "The absorption and dissociative or ionizing effect of monochromatic radiation in an atmosphere on a rotating earth. Part II. Grazing incidence," *Proceedings of the Physical Society* (1931).

This is the classic Chapman-layer source for photoionization structure.

## Lorenz 1963

[Lorenz 1963](#lorenz-1963): Edward N. Lorenz, "Deterministic Nonperiodic Flow," *Journal of the Atmospheric Sciences* 20(2), 130-141 (1963).

This is the canonical reference for sensitive dependence on initial conditions in atmospheric dynamics.

## Berner 2017

[Berner et al. 2017](#berner-2017): Judith Berner et al., "Stochastic parameterization: Toward a new view of weather and climate models," *Bulletin of the American Meteorological Society* / arXiv review (2017).

This is a useful reference for stochastic closures and ensemble-based uncertainty representation.

## McGranaghan 2020

[McGranaghan et al. 2020](#mcgranaghan-2020): Ryan M. McGranaghan et al., "Toward a Next Generation Particle Precipitation Model: Mesoscale Prediction Through Machine Learning (a Case Study and Framework for Progress)," arXiv (2020).

This provides modern context for particle precipitation modeling and space-weather prediction.

## Grandin 2023

[Grandin et al. 2023](#grandin-2023): Maxime Grandin et al., "First 3D hybrid-Vlasov global simulation of auroral proton precipitation and comparison with satellite observations," arXiv (2023).

This gives a direct geospace context for precipitation-driven ionospheric forcing.

## Schmid 2010

[Schmid 2010](#schmid-2010): Peter J. Schmid, "Dynamic mode decomposition of numerical and experimental data," *Journal of Fluid Mechanics* 656(1), 5-28 (2010).

This is the standard DMD reference used for Figure 3.

## Tu 2014

[Tu et al. 2014](#tu-2014): Jonathan H. Tu, Clarence W. Rowley, Dirk M. Luchtenburg, Steven L. Brunton, and J. Nathan Kutz, "On Dynamic Mode Decomposition: Theory and Applications," *Journal of Computational Dynamics* 1(2), 391-421 (2014).

This is the common theoretical reference for DMD and Koopman-style interpretation.

## Mezić 2005

[Mezić 2005](#mezic-2005): Igor Mezić, "Spectral properties of dynamical systems, model reduction and decompositions," *Nonlinear Dynamics* 41, 309-325 (2005).

This is the Koopman-theory reference used to justify operator-based reduced modeling.

## Higham 2001

[Higham 2001](#higham-2001): Desmond J. Higham, "An algorithmic introduction to numerical simulation of stochastic differential equations," *SIAM Review* 43(3), 525-546 (2001).

This is a standard reference for Euler-Maruyama simulation.

## Oksendal 2003

[Øksendal 2003](#oksendal-2003): Bernt Øksendal, *Stochastic Differential Equations* (Springer, 2003).

This is a general SDE reference for the stochastic forcing formalism.

## Chirikov 1979

[Chirikov 1979](#chirikov-1979): B. V. Chirikov, "A universal instability of many-dimensional oscillator systems," *Physics Reports* 52(5), 263-379 (1979).

This is the original resonance-overlap criterion often cited in chaos studies.

## Vassiliadis 1990

[Vassiliadis et al. 1990](#vassiliadis-1990): D. Vassiliadis et al., *Geophysical Research Letters* 17, 1841-1844 (1990).

This is one of the classic low-dimensional-chaos studies of geomagnetic indices.

## Baker 1990

[Baker et al. 1990](#baker-1990): D. N. Baker et al., *Geophysical Research Letters* 17, 41-44 (1990).

This is another early low-dimensional-chaos result for geomagnetic variability.

## Sharma 1995

[Sharma 1995](#sharma-1995): A. S. Sharma, *Reviews of Geophysics* 33, 645-650 (1995).

This is a review of driven nonlinear oscillator descriptions for magnetospheric dynamics.

## Chapman 1998

[Chapman et al. 1998](#chapman-1998): S. C. Chapman et al., *Geophysical Research Letters* 25, 2397-2400 (1998).

This is a commonly cited SOC-style substorm avalanche reference.

## Consolini 1997

[Consolini 1997](#consolini-1997): G. Consolini, *Cosmic Physics in the Year 2000* (1997).

This is a sandpile/SOC-style magnetospheric model reference.

## Bak 1987

[Bak, Tang & Wiesenfeld 1987](#bak-1987): P. Bak, C. Tang, and K. Wiesenfeld, *Physical Review Letters* 59, 381-384 (1987).

This is the original self-organized criticality concept.

## Ukhorskiy 2013

[Ukhorskiy & Sitnov 2013](#ukhorskiy-2013): A. Yu. Ukhorskiy and M. I. Sitnov, *Space Science Reviews* 179, 545-578 (2013).

This reviews Chirikov-style resonance overlap and its connection to chaotic precipitation.

## Lu 1991

[Lu & Hamilton 1991](#lu-1991): E. T. Lu and R. J. Hamilton, *The Astrophysical Journal* 380, L89-L92 (1991).

This is the classical solar-flare SOC avalanche reference.

## Wheatland 2000

[Wheatland 2000](#wheatland-2000): M. S. Wheatland, *The Astrophysical Journal* 536(2) (2000).

This is an observational flare waiting-time reference often used with SOC discussions.

## Riley 2012

[Riley 2012](#riley-2012): P. Riley, *Space Weather* 10, S02012 (2012).

This is a reference for heavy-tailed extreme storm-amplitude statistics.

## Uritsky 1998

[Uritsky & Pudovkin 1998](#uritsky-1998): V. M. Uritsky and M. I. Pudovkin, *Annales Geophysicae* 16, 1580-1588 (1998).

This is an AE-index 1/f-fluctuation and SOC-related companion reference.

## Morina 2019

[Morina et al. 2019](#morina-2019): D. Morina, E. Serra, P. Puig, and A. Corral, *Scientific Reports* 9, 2393 (2019).

This is a recent Carrington-class extreme-storm probability estimate.
